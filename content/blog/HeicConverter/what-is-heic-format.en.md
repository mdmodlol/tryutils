# What is HEIC Format? Why Do iPhone Photos Use HEIC?

If you've used an iPhone, you might have noticed when exporting photos to your computer that their file extension isn't the common **.jpg**, but rather **.heic**. So, what is HEIC format? Why did Apple choose it as the default image storage method?

---

## What is HEIC?

HEIC, which stands for **High Efficiency Image Container**, is an image file format based on **HEIF (High Efficiency Image Format)**.  
It was introduced by Apple as the default photo format starting with **iOS 11 and macOS High Sierra**.

In simple terms: **HEIC = A more space-efficient JPG alternative**.

---

## Advantages of HEIC

1. **Smaller File Size**  
   - At the same image quality, HEIC files are approximately **50%** the size of JPG files.  
   - This means you can store more photos on your iPhone without filling up storage.  

2. **Higher Image Quality**  
   - HEIC supports 16-bit color depth, while JPG only supports 8-bit.  
   - This results in more natural color transitions and better detail in highlights and shadows.  

3. **Support for More Features**  
   - Live Photos  
   - Multiple images packaged in the same file  
   - Transparency channel (similar to PNG)  

---

## Disadvantages of HEIC

1. **Poor Compatibility**  
   - Older versions of Windows, some Android devices, and certain websites may not support HEIC.  
   - This is why many people need to convert HEIC to JPG or PNG.  

2. **Limited Editing Tool Support**  
   - While Photoshop/Lightroom and other major software now support HEIC, some niche applications may not be able to open it.  

---

## Why Does iPhone Use HEIC?

Apple chose HEIC for two main reasons:

1. **Storage Optimization**  
   - iPhone camera resolution keeps increasing, and continuing to use JPG would quickly fill up storage space.  
   - HEIC can significantly reduce space usage while maintaining high image quality.  

2. **Technology Leadership**  
   - Apple has always aimed to promote more advanced multimedia standards (like early adoption of AAC audio).  
   - HEIC is an **ISO standard** and is expected to potentially replace JPG in the future.
